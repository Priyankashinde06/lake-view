Attractions

Kaas Plateau

Known as Maharashtra's Valley of Flowers, this UNESCO World Heritage Site blooms with over 850 species of wildflowers during monsoon season (August-October). The colorful floral carpet against the lateritic rock formations creates a breathtaking landscape just 25km from Satara.

Read More

Thoseghar Waterfalls

Located 30km from Satara, these spectacular waterfalls cascade from 500m height during monsoon. The main waterfall is surrounded by about 15 smaller falls, creating a misty wonderland amidst lush greenery from July to October.

This picturesque hill station (45km from Satara) is famous for its strawberry farms, colonial-era boarding schools, and stunning viewpoints like Table Land (Asia's second-largest volcanic plateau). Enjoy paragliding, horseback riding, and panoramic views of five surrounding valleys from 1,334m elevation.

Sajjangad

This historic hill fort 15km from Satara is the final resting place of Saint Ramdas. The spiritual site offers panoramic views of the Sahyadri mountains and features ancient temples, meditation caves, and the original samadhi of the 17th century saint.
